<div><h2 id="the-evolution-of-distributed-tracing">The Evolution of Distributed Tracing</h2></div>
<blockquote>
<p>Distributed tracing (also known as distributed request tracing) is a method used to analyze and monitor applications, especially those built using microservices architecture. Distributed tracing helps pinpoint where failures occur and what causes poor performance.</p>
</blockquote>
<div><h3 id="origins">Origins</h3></div>
<p>The term “Distributed Tracing” first appeared in the paper <strong>《Dapper, a Large-Scale Distributed Systems Tracing Infrastructure》</strong> published by Google. This paper has had a profound influence on the design concepts of later open-source distributed tracing projects such as Jaeger and Zipkin.</p>
<p>Microservices architecture is a distributed architecture with many different services. Different services call each other, and if an error occurs, a single request may pass through N services. As business grows, the number of service calls increases. Without a tool to record the call chain, solving problems becomes like untangling a ball of yarn played by a kitten in the image below—utterly confusing and difficult to start.
<img alt="image.png" src="https://p3-juejin.byteimg.com/tos-cn-i-k3u1fbpfcp/e2dd5606765649969819396ba574a741~tplv-k3u1fbpfcp-watermark.image" />
Therefore, a tool is needed to clearly understand which services a request has passed through and in what order, making it easy to locate issues.
<img alt="image.png" src="https://p9-juejin.byteimg.com/tos-cn-i-k3u1fbpfcp/7098634cefe74a3cbacf5e76c343bd81~tplv-k3u1fbpfcp-watermark.image" /></p>
<div><h3 id="a-flourishing-of-tools">A Flourishing of Tools</h3></div>
<p>After Google released <strong>Dapper</strong>, more and more distributed tracing tools emerged. Below are some commonly used distributed tracing systems:</p>
<ul>
<li>Skywalking</li>
<li>Alibaba Eagle Eye</li>
<li>Dianping CAT</li>
<li>Twitter Zipkin</li>
<li>Naver Pinpoint</li>
<li>Uber Jaeger</li>
</ul>
<div><h3 id="rivalry">Rivalry?</h3></div>
<p>As the number of tracing tools increased, the open-source community was mainly divided into two factions. One faction followed the <strong>OpenTracing</strong> specification led by the <strong>CNCF Technical Committee</strong>, such as Jaeger and Zipkin, which adhere to the <strong>OpenTracing</strong> specification. The other faction was <strong>OpenCensus</strong>, initiated by Google. Google, being the first company to propose the concept of distributed tracing, was later joined by Microsoft in <strong>OpenCensus</strong>.
<img alt="截屏2021-05-29 下午9.56.57.png" src="https://p3-juejin.byteimg.com/tos-cn-i-k3u1fbpfcp/3029f1315fe34ec884858d33d41cb1ce~tplv-k3u1fbpfcp-watermark.image" /></p>
<div><h3 id="the-birth-of-opentelemetry">The Birth of OpenTelemetry</h3></div>
<blockquote>
<p>OpenTelemetry is a set of APIs, SDKs, modules, and integrations designed for creating and managing‎‎telemetry data‎‎(such as traces, metrics, and logs).</p>
</blockquote>
<p>After Microsoft joined <strong>OpenCensus</strong>, it directly disrupted the previous balance, indirectly leading to the birth of <strong>OpenTelemetry</strong>.
Google and Microsoft were determined to end the chaos in the community. The primary issue was how to integrate the existing projects from both communities. The main concept of OpenTelemetry is to be compatible with both <strong>OpenCensus</strong> and <strong>OpenTracing</strong>, allowing users to integrate <strong>OpenTelemetry</strong> with little or no modification.</p>
<div><h2 id="kratos-distributed-tracing-practice">Kratos Distributed Tracing Practice</h2></div>
<blockquote>
<p>Kratos is a lightweight Go microservices framework that includes a large number of microservices-related frameworks and tools.</p>
</blockquote>
<div><h3 id="tracing-middleware">Tracing Middleware</h3></div>
<p>Among the built-in middleware provided by the Kratos framework, there is a <strong>tracing</strong> middleware that implements distributed tracing functionality based on <strong>OpenTelemetry</strong>. The middleware code can be found at <strong><a href="https://github.com/go-kratos/kratos/tree/main/middleware/tracing">middleware/tracing</a></strong>.</p>
